1. What this monitor does

1. Yeh monitor kya karta hai?

ECG NIBP SpO₂ Respiratory Rate Temperature Early Warning Score (NEWS)

The GE B105M is a multi-parameter bedside monitor commonly used in Emergency, ICU and HDU. It displays heart rate and rhythm (ECG), non-invasive blood pressure, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate and temperature. It also includes a built-in Early Warning Score (National Early Warning Score – NEWS) calculator.

GE B105M ek multi-parameter bedside monitor hai jo Emergency, ICU aur HDU mein use hota hai. Isme ECG se heart rate & rhythm, non-invasive BP, SpO₂, respiratory rate aur temperature dikhai deta hai. Iske andar Early Warning Score (NEWS) ka calculator bhi built-in hai.

This page is for teaching and training only. Always follow your hospital policy and senior clinical decision-making.

2. Basic setup for a new patient

2. Naye patient par monitor ka basic setup

A. Safety checks before connecting

A. Patient ko connect karne se pehle safety checks

  • Confirm patient identity with name and identifier (UHID / IP number).
  • Ensure the monitor and trolley are plugged into a safe power source.
  • Check that all cables are intact (no cuts, exposed wires, broken pins).
  • Clean ECG electrodes and SpO₂ probe as per infection control policy.
  • Patient ki pehchaan confirm karein – naam aur UHID / IP number.
  • Monitor trolley ko safe power socket mein plug karein.
  • Saare cables dekhlein – kahin tar kati hui, pin tooti hui ya exposed wire na ho.
  • ECG electrodes aur SpO₂ probe ko infection control policy ke hisaab se clean rakhein.

B. Connecting the patient

B. Patient ko monitor se kaise jodein

  1. Apply 3-lead or 5-lead ECG as per hospital standard and connect to the ECG cable.
  2. Wrap the NIBP cuff snugly on the upper arm (cuff size should match arm circumference).
  3. Place SpO₂ probe on finger or toe, avoiding nail polish or strong ambient light.
  4. If available, connect temperature probe and place correctly (oral/axillary/rectal as policy).
  5. Press the main power button once to wake the monitor if it is in standby.
  1. Hospital policy ke hisaab se 3-lead ya 5-lead ECG lagayein aur ECG cable se connect karein.
  2. NIBP cuff ko arm par snug fit rakhein – cuff size arm circumference ke hisaab se sahi ho.
  3. SpO₂ probe finger ya toe par lagayein, nail polish ya bahut bright light se bachein.
  4. Agar temperature probe available ho to use connect karein aur sahi jagah par lagayein (oral/axillary/rectal as per policy).
  5. Agar monitor standby par ho to power button dabakar on karein.

3. Using the built-in Early Warning Score (NEWS)

3. Built-in Early Warning Score (NEWS) ka istemaal

A. Parameters used in NEWS

A. NEWS mein kaun-kaun se parameters aate hain?

  • Respiratory rate (RR)
  • Oxygen saturation (SpO₂)
  • Air or oxygen (patient on supplemental O₂ or room air)
  • Systolic blood pressure (SBP)
  • Pulse / heart rate
  • Level of consciousness (AVPU: Alert, Voice, Pain, Unresponsive)
  • Temperature
  • Respiratory rate (saans ki ginti – RR)
  • Oxygen saturation (SpO₂)
  • Air ya oxygen (patient sirf room air par hai ya O₂ support par)
  • Systolic BP (SBP)
  • Pulse / heart rate
  • Consciousness level – AVPU (Alert, Voice, Pain, Unresponsive)
  • Temperature

B. Step-by-step on the monitor

B. Monitor par step-by-step kaise karein

  1. Open the Early Warning Score or EWS/NEWS screen from the monitor menu.
  2. Allow the monitor to take a fresh NIBP reading and ensure SpO₂ and HR are stable.
  3. Check that RR and temperature are displayed; if not, measure manually and enter the value.
  4. In the NEWS screen, use the arrows or numeric fields to enter:
    • RR, SpO₂, SBP, pulse, temperature
    • Whether the patient is on oxygen
    • Consciousness level (AVPU)
  5. The monitor automatically calculates sub-scores and shows a total NEWS score.
  6. Check the risk band and follow the escalation guidance shown on the “Guidance” tab.
  1. Monitor ke menu se Early Warning Score / EWS / NEWS wala screen open karein.
  2. NIBP ka ek fresh reading lein, SpO₂ aur HR stable hone den.
  3. Dekhein ki RR aur temperature display ho rahe hain; agar nahi to manually measure karke value daalein.
  4. NEWS screen mein arrows / number field se yeh values bhar dein:
    • RR, SpO₂, SBP, pulse, temperature
    • Patient O₂ par hai ya sirf room air par
    • Consciousness level – AVPU
  5. Monitor khud-ba-khud har parameter ka sub-score aur total NEWS score dikha dega.
  6. Risk band dekhein aur “Guidance” tab mein diya hua escalation plan follow karein.

C. NEWS score bands and monitoring plan

C. NEWS score bands aur monitoring ka plan

NEWS score Risk band Suggested monitoring & action
0 Low Routine monitoring, at least 12-hourly observations if patient is stable.
1–4 Low–medium Increase frequency to at least 4–6-hourly. Review for cause of abnormality.
Score of 3 in any single parameter Single-parameter concern Minimum 1-hourly observations and urgent review by a clinician.
≥5 (total) Urgent response Minimum 1-hourly monitoring; urgent assessment by ED/ICU doctor, plan for escalation.
≥7 (total) Emergency response Continuous monitoring of vitals; immediate senior review, consider ICU transfer / Code Blue.
NEWS score Risk level Monitoring & action (Hinglish)
0 Low Routine observation. Stable patient ke liye kam se kam 12 ghante mein ek full vitals set.
1–4 Low–Medium Observation 4–6 ghante mein ek baar. Score kyun badh raha hai – reason check karein.
Kisi bhi ek parameter ka score 3 Warning sign Har 1 ghante mein vitals repeat karein. Duty doctor / registrar ko urgent bataayein.
≥5 (total) Urgent Kam se kam 1-hourly monitoring. ED / ICU doctor se turant review aur escalation plan banayein.
≥7 (total) Emergency Continuous monitoring chalu rakhein. Senior doctor ko turant bulaayein, ICU transfer / Code Blue consider karein.

NEWS is designed for adult patients. For children, pregnancy, or specific conditions, follow local paediatric and obstetric early warning systems.

NEWS mainly adult patients ke liye bana hai. Bachchon, pregnancy ya special conditions mein hamesha hospital ke paediatric / obstetric early warning charts follow karein.

4. Alarms, troubleshooting and good practice

4. Alarms, common problems aur good practice

A. Alarm basics

A. Alarm kaise set karein

  • Use age-appropriate limits for HR, RR, BP and SpO₂ according to hospital policy.
  • Never silence alarms permanently. If an alarm is silenced during a procedure, turn it back on immediately afterwards.
  • Document any persistent abnormal values and inform the responsible doctor.
  • HR, RR, BP aur SpO₂ ke alarm limits hospital policy ke hisaab se set karein (adult / paediatric).
  • Alarms ko kabhi permanently off mat rakhein. Procedure ke time agar mute kiya ho to turant wapas on karein.
  • Jo bhi value baar-baar abnormal aa rahi ho usko notes mein likh kar doctor ko inform karein.

B. Common issues & fixes

B. Common problems aur unke quick fixes

  • SpO₂ probe off / poor signal: Check probe position, warm the extremity, avoid shivering and bright light, change probe site if needed.
  • ECG noisy / wandering baseline: Re-attach electrodes, clean skin, avoid leads crossing mains cables, check for patient movement or shivering.
  • NIBP not reading: Ensure cuff tubing is not kinked, cuff is the correct size and tightly wrapped; avoid measuring on an arm with IV infusion or fistula.
  • RR not displaying: Confirm that the correct module is selected; otherwise measure manually and enter RR in the NEWS screen.
  • “SpO₂ probe off” / weak signal: Probe ki position theek karein, haath/paon garam rakhein, shivering kam karein, zarurat ho to site change karein.
  • ECG mein noise / line hil rahi hai: Electrodes dobara chipkaayein, skin clean karein, ECG wires ko power cables se door rakhein, patient movement control karein.
  • NIBP reading nahi aa rahi: Cuff tube me kink na ho, cuff size sahi ho aur tight bandha ho, IV line wale ya fistula arm par measure na karein.
  • RR screen par nahi aa raha: Sahi module select hai ya nahi check karein; agar available na ho to RR manually ginke NEWS screen mein enter karein.

5. Quick workflow for new users (summary)

5. Naye user ke liye quick workflow (short mein)

  1. Identify patient → connect ECG, SpO₂, NIBP (and temperature probe if available).
  2. Start monitoring and ensure waveforms / numbers look reasonable.
  3. Open the Early Warning Score (NEWS) screen.
  4. Enter RR, SpO₂, SBP, pulse, consciousness (AVPU), temperature and oxygen therapy.
  5. Read the total NEWS score and risk band.
  6. Plan monitoring frequency and escalation according to the score and hospital policy.
  7. Document values, NEWS score and actions taken in the clinical notes / nursing chart.
  1. Patient ki identity confirm karein → ECG, SpO₂, NIBP (aur temp probe) connect karein.
  2. Monitor par waveforms / numbers ko check karein – kuch bhi “abnormal bizarre” lage to dobara leads dekhein.
  3. Early Warning Score / NEWS wala screen open karein.
  4. RR, SpO₂, SBP, pulse, AVPU, temperature aur O₂ therapy ki information fill karein.
  5. Total NEWS score aur risk band padhein.
  6. Score ke hisaab se monitoring kitni baar karni hai aur kis level par escalate karna hai yeh decide karein (hospital policy follow karke).
  7. Saare vitals, NEWS score aur jo action liya (fluid, review, ICU call etc.) notes / nursing chart mein likhein.
